Natalia Benetti 0001-01-01 2 min read

Maternal SMCHD1 regulates Hox gene expression and patterning in the mouse embryo Structural maintenance of chromosomes flexible hinge domain containing 1 (SMCHD1) is a non-canonical SMC protein that plays a key role in X chromosome inactivation and the epigenetic silencing of several clustered gene families, including the Hox clusters. Jansz et al., found that zygotic Smchd1-null mouse embryos displayed precocious activation of the Hox genes responsible for posterior vertebral patterning and consequential posterior-shift homeotic transformations in their skeletons.

Recently, we found that SMCHD1 is also a maternal effect gene, where the embryo is under the control of maternal SMCHD1 until the 16-32 cell stage of development (Wanigasuriya, Gouil et al., 2020), so the only SMCHD1 protein present in the zygote until that point is supplied by the oocyte. We have now found that maternal SMCHD1 influences Hox gene expression and embryo patterning. We have found that E17.5 embryos which lack maternal Smchd1 exhibit highly penetrant shift homeotic transformations in axial patterning. RNA-sequencing of E8.5 tailbud tissue and an ES cell to neuromesodermal progenitor differentiation series of Smchd1 wildtype and maternal null embryos show a modest but consistent upregulation of anterior Hox genes.

To assess how maternal SMCHD1 is able to regulate Hox gene expression post-implantation, carried our CUT&RUN for the Polycomb repressive complex marks H3K27me3 and H2AK119ub in our ESC model. We found no difference in the coverage density of these chromatin marks over the Hox clusters between Smchd1 wildtype and maternal null ESC lines, indicating that maternal Smchd1 does not act upstream of Polycomb and may rather act downstream. Taken together our data suggests a model where maternal SMCHD1 sets up a chromatin state preimplantation that is required for appropriate Hox gene expression post-implantation. We are now investigating how this may occur at the molecular level.
