The use of non-invasive imaging biomarkers to predict survival outcomes in pancreatic cancer: A digital innovation project from the PURPLE pancreatic cancer platform

Background: Pancreatic ductal adenocarcinoma (PDAC) is known for its poor prognostic outcomes due to frequently advanced stage at diagnosis and treatment resistance. Recently radiomics has shown promise as a biomarker in PDAC. By introducing a quantitative measure such imaging biomarkers have potential to be incorporated into prognostic models.

Material & Methods: Between 2016 and 2021, de-identified data and staging CTs were extracted from the PURPLE pancreatic cancer registry, for PDAC patients treated at Peter MacCallum Cancer Centre and Royal Melbourne Hospital. We evaluated radiomic feature (RF) robustness based on slice thickeness, 2D and 3D segmentation and 41 features were extracted with LIFEx software. CT imaging was reconstructed into 1mm, 3mm and 5mm slice thickness. Intraclass correlation coefficient (ICC) evaluated slice thickness robustness of each RF. Robust features were correlated with clinical and survival outcomes via univariate and multivariate models.

Results: Ninety-eight patients with PDAC (50 females; mean age 68 years) were evaluated. 42 had early-stage disease (HR=1, p<0.001), 28 had advanced unresectable (HR=1.942, p=0.023) and 28 had metastatic disease (HR=4.083, p<0.001) at initial diagnosis. CT reconstruction resulted in 36 (1mm), 96 (3mm) and 98 (5mm) slice thicknesses, respectively. GLRLM_HGRE was the only overlapping feature in the top ranked ICC comparison between 2D (0.994, 95%CI 0.990-0.997) and 3D (0.981, 95%CI 0.964-0.990). None of the 2D RFs showed significant correlation with overall survival in Cox-regression model. In 3D analysis, one RFs were significantly associated with overall survival:GLZLM_SZHGE (HR=2.052, p=0.016). Further radiomic modelling is underway to determine the prognostic value of these non-invasive radiomic biomarkers and to further correlate these with clinical and histological features.

Conclusion: The higher order 3D radiomic features provided a significant correlation to overall survival which could be used as imaging biomarkers. Further analysis is underway to expand the cohort and better determine the prognostic impact of these findings.
