EVALUATING THE THERAPEUTIC POTENTIAL OF TBK1 INHIBITION IN CANCER IMMUNOTHERAPY-ASSOCIATED RHEUMATIC ADVERSE EVENTS

INTRODUCTION

Immune checkpoint inhibitors (ICIs) are a highly efficacious class of cancer immunotherapy but have been associated with the development of autoimmune-like rheumatic adverse events (RAEs). RAEs are currently treated with broadly immunosuppressive corticosteroids but these may compromise the anti-tumour effect of ICIs. Thus, new therapies that can resolve RAEs without negating the benefit of ICIs are urgently needed. TANK-binding kinase 1 (TBK1) could be an attractive target for inhibition in RAE due to its dual roles in autoimmunity and anti-tumour responses. Here, we investigate the therapeutic potential of TBK1i as a new anti-rheumatic therapy for autoimmunity, particularly in RAE setting.

METHODS

To examine the immune basis of ICI-associated RAEs, arthritis-resistant C57BL6/J mice were induced with CIA and administered with either isotype control or combination anti-PD-1 and anti-CTLA-4 ICI antibodies. To examine the effect of TBK1i in autoimmune settings, arthritis-prone DBA/1 mice were induced for CIA and given vehicle, prednisolone or TBK1i. At endpoint, lymphoid organs were collected for flow cytometric analysis of autoimmunity-associated immune cells such as T follicular helper (Tfh), T regulatory (Treg), germinal centre (GC) B, IgG1 B and plasma cells.

RESULTS

ICI treatment resulted in the expansion of lymph node Tfh, GC B and plasma cells, indicating amplified humoral autoimmune responses. As expected, prednisolone depleted all immune cell subsets indicative of broad immunosuppression in the CIA model. In contrast, TBK1i selectively reduced the proportion of plasma cells while retaining other immune cell subsets.

CONCLUSION

ICI administration resulted in the expansion of humoral autoimmunity-associated immune cells, which may be associated with enhanced pathogenic autoantibody production and therefore, the development of some RAE subset in patients. TBK1i could block autoimmunity and RAE by limiting the expansion of pathogenic autoantibody-producing plasma cells. Future work will examine other autoimmune-associated immune cells and the effect of TBK1i on anti-tumour immunity.
