Targeting Podoplanin with CAR T cells to treat Diffuse Midline Glioma

Background: Diffuse Midline Glioma (DMG) is a heterogenous and currently incurable paediatric brain tumour. Chimeric Antigen Receptor (CAR) T cells have been used to treat DMG, however antigen escape in clinical trials indicates that discovery of novel immunotherapeutic targets is essential for more robust therapies. Recently, enrichment for surface expressed proteins in primary DMG tumour sample transcriptomes identified Podoplanin overexpression. Though previously characterised in carcinomas and gliomas, indication of this mucin-like glycoprotein in DMG is novel and may be targetable.



Methods: Flow cytometry was used to screen for endogenous Podoplanin surface expression on glioma cell lines and surgically removed patient tumour samples. Retroviral transduction of tagged human recombinant Podoplanin and Fluorescence Activated Cell Sorting generated high Podoplanin expressing cell models. Podoplanin antibody heavy and light chain sequences from patents were re-engineered into myc-tagged short chain variable fragments in silico and then cloned into a previously validated 2nd generation CAR scaffold. 7 novel Podoplanin CARs were each retrovirally transduced into CD8+ T cells isolated from C57Bl/6 mice. CAR T cell in vitro functional characterisation required flow cytometry, incucyte killing assays and cytokine bead arrays (CBA).


Results: DMG models SU-DIPG25, SU-DIPG33 and SU-DIPG38 displayed detectable Podoplanin by flow cytometry, but not by previous mass spectrometry. Interestingly, a glioblastoma multiforme surgical sample cultured into a cell line displayed high Podoplanin expression 7 weeks post surgery, however, was undetectable by 13 weeks. Although cocultures with Podoplanin CAR T cells and Podoplanin expressing HEK293T cells demonstrated no cytotoxicity in incucyte killing assays (n=2), CBA suggested CAR stimulated IFN-g release for CAR T cells derived from 2 distinct Podoplanin antibody clones (p<0.05, n=1).

Conclusion: Podoplanin expression in DMG primary tissue and cell lines suggests an uncharacterised indication in the paediatric glioma setting. Refinement of Podoplanin CAR design to improve tumour killing in vitro warrants further investigation.
