Understanding the regulation of the pro-apoptotic BH3-only protein PUMA to enhance the efficacy of cancer treatment

Background: Apoptosis is a genetically programmed process of cell death that is dysregulated in diverse cancers. The BCL-2 (B-cell lymphoma 2) protein family regulates the mitochondrial- mediated process of apoptosis, and it contains both pro-survival and pro-apoptotic family members. BH3 (Bcl-2 homolog region [BH] 3)-only proteins (e.g. BIM, PUMA, NOXA) are pro-apoptotic members of the BCL-2 protein family that initiate the process of apoptosis, and play crucial roles in the killing of malignant cells by diverse anti-cancer agents. Several chemotherapeutics kill malignant cells by activation of the tumour suppressor p53. The BH3- only protein PUMA is a critical mediator of both p53-dependent and independent apoptosis and interacts with all the pro-survival BCL-2 family members (BCL-2, MCL-1, BCL-XL, A1/BFL-1, BCL-W). The absence of PUMA can accelerate tumour development and increase therapy resistance in malignant cells.Â Our research aims to understand the regulation of PUMA by p53- dependent and independent mechanisms to find novel ways to boost expression of PUMA in malignant cells to enhance the efficacy of standard-of-care anti-cancer therapies.

Methods: We have engineered a novel reporter mouse that has the tdTomato gene replacing the PUMA encoding region. We will generate mouse models of blood cancer using cells from the reporter mice and use them to monitor PUMA gene induction in malignant cells using FACS and microscopy. We will also identify novel regulators of PUMA expression by performing CRISPR/Cas9 whole genome knock-out and activation screens.

Results: We have generated Acute Myeloid Leukemia (AML) tumours from PUMA/Bbc reporter hematopoietic stem cells and have derived cell lines from the tumours. Upregulated expression of PUMA in the AML cells expressing the PUMA reporter can be detected after treating the cells with diverse anti-cancer drugs. Furthermore, we have identified candidate genes that can induce or repress PUMA gene expression by carrying out a CRISPR gene knockout screen.

Conclusion: The BH3-only protein PUMA is essential for the initiation of apoptotic cell death through the mitochondrial (aka intrinsic or BCL-2-regulated) pathway.
