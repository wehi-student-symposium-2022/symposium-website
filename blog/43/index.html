Leveraging a novel combinational therapy as a treatment for lupus

Current treatments for the chronic autoimmune disease Systemic Lupus Erythematosus (lupus) are either non-specific or do not control inflammation, meaning that refractory disease often occurs. In lupus, the production and deposition of autoantibodies triggers inflammation in organs such as the kidneys and skin. Therapeutic strategies which target both pathogenic plasma cells and inflammation are required to not only control lupus symptoms but to inhibit disease progression.

The proteasome inhibitor, Bortezomib, effectively depletes plasma cells. Early-disease lupus mice treated with Bortezomib demonstrated peripheral plasma cell deletion, disease prevention and remission. Unfortunately, Bortezomib treated lupus patients experienced adverse events, and late-stage diseased lupus mice suffered lethality associated with elevated levels of the proinflammatory cytokine, interleukin 6 (IL-6). Thus, while Bortezomib efficiently depletes pathogenic plasma cells in lupus, inflammation needs to be controlled to make this a viable treatment.

The Janus Kinase (JAK)-inhibitor, Ruxolitinib, inhibits JAK1 and JAK2 which are required for activation of certain JAK-signal and activator of transcription (STAT) pathways. Ruxolitinib treatment represses activity of some pro-inflammatory cytokines, including IL-6. We hypothesise Ruxolitinib could be administered in combination with Bortezomib to control inflammation and deplete plasma cells.

MRL/lpr mice which express lupus-like disease were treated with vehicle, single treatments or the combined treatment of Ruxolitinib and Bortezomib over 4 weeks. The frequency of circulating immune cells, particularly plasma cells were assessed by FACS, inflammatory cytokines and autoantibodies in the serum were assessed by ELISA and hallmarks of lupus disease scored by histopathology.

Preliminary data indicates that combined bortezomib and ruxolitinib treatment reduces serum autoantibodies and protects MRL/lpr mice from Bortezomib induced weight loss. We predict that combination treatment will decrease plasma cell frequencies and inflammatory cytokines levels. If proved efficacious, Bortezomib and Ruxolitinib combination treatment could become a viable treatment option for lupus and may provide insight into other plasma cell driven pathologies.
