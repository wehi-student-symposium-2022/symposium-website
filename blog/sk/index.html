Investigating enhancer regulation of lineage specification in the mammary gland

Transcriptional regulation governs lineage differentiation in all tissues including the mammary gland, yet our understanding of this regulatory system remains limited. Enhancer regulation of transcriptional programs are emerging as critical to the differentiation process and cancer. Transcription factors also coordinate lineage specificity and mutations in transcription factor networks are associated with cancer. Lineage specification is important in breast cancer development because breast cancer sub types arise from cells along different stages of the differentiation hierarchy. It was hypothesized that enhancers of lineage specific transcription factors are critical to the differentiation of basal and luminal lineages in the mammary gland. Activating CRISPRa was used to interrogate the role of transcription factor enhancers in lineage differentiation and cancer development. Multiple CRISPRa systems were used to activate putative enhancer regions in the murine mammary epithelial comma-dgeo cell line. The effects of this enhancer activation on target gene transcription and expression were analysed by western blot and qPCR. Two SAM CRISPRa systems were compared for enhancer activation efficiency and showed varied results in target gene upregulation. Evidence suggested that the triple vector SAM CRISPRa system was most efficient at enhancer activation. Comma-dgeo cells showed stable dCas9 and guide expression but unstable transactivator expression with the triple vector SAM system in culture. Activating putative enhancers for dNp63, Msx2 and Elf5 showed increased target gene expression in some experiments. Future directions of the project include continuing functional validation of enhancers, phenotyping of CRISPRa enhancer cell lines and activating lineage specific enhancers in primary mammary epithelial cells.
