Timely personalization of colorectal cancer patient treatment using low viscosity tumoroid suspension cultures

Patient-derived tumoroid (PDT) is a promising tool for personalizing cancer treatment. However, implement of PDTs in clinical therapeutic selection is impeded by the lack of standardization in PDT generation and drug testing. Here, we evaluated the feasibility of implementing LVM CRC PDTs with clinical decision making. 106 PDTs from 104 CRC patients were grown via low-viscosity suspension culture method developed by our group (named LVM CRC PDTs). Genomic features and histopathological features were similar between LVM CRC PDTs and original tumors. When screened against 5FU, SN38, oxaliplatin, regorafenib, TAS102, erlotinib, FOLFIRI and FOLFOX, PDT drug responses showed that LVM CRC PDTs recapitulated known markers in CRC treatment, including associations of KRAS mutation with resistance to anti-EGFR treatment. PDT sensitivity to a given treatment was defined by the response rates in clinical trials, and LVM CRC PDTs showed high accuracy in predicting patient responses. Moreover, we found that dose-titrations could be limited to representative points/windows whilst retaining assay accuracy, reducing the required input number of cells. In conclusions, we have developed a robust method for evaluating PDT drug responses and accurately predicting CRC patient responses to chemotherapies and target therapies adaptable to the timeframe of clinical decision-making.
